Genome-Wide Association Plot for Schizophrenia
  • As you can see on the Manhattan plot, the strongest association with schizophrenia by far appears on loci within chromosome 6.
  • There are associations above the threshold on nearly every chromosome, supporting the idea that schizophrenia is a very polygenic trait.
  • Chromosomes 9 and 21 show fewer associations above the significance threshold in this dataset.
  • The very high peak in chromosome 6 suggests that a cluster of SNPs on this chromosome are in strong linkage disequilbrium with each other, and they have them same underlying locus driving the signal.

Biological Pathway Enrichment: What Do These Genes Do Together?
  • 15 pathways reached statistical significance after multiple-testing correction (adjusted p < 0.05).
  • Calcium signaling, neuronal development, and synaptic function are the dominant biological themes.
  • Cardiac pathways (arrhythmia, cardiomyopathy) reflect shared ion channel genes expressed in both heart and brain; not evidence of cardiac involvement in schizophrenia.
  • Look at the enrichment bubble plot. In the Calcium Ion Import pathway, 4 of 12 genes in this pathway (33%) have a variant associated with schizophrenia.
  • Look at the enrichment bubble plot. In the Regulation of Synapse Disassembly pathway, 2 of 8 genes in this pathway (25%) have a variant associated with schizophrenia.
  • The HLA pathway we have discussed in other sections is excluded here; it is already well-established to be implicated with schizophrenia and there are computational limitations of getting it to hit on pathway enrichment.
